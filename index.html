Solar System

The Solar System is an astronomical structure formed by eight planets, plus several other celestial bodies, orbiting the Sun. Its origin, according to various scientific studies, was made possible by the collapse of a cloud of celestial objects. The Solar System consists of eight planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune. Pluto, previously classified as a planet, has been downgraded to a dwarf planet. Besides the planets, there are several celestial bodies that are part of our system, such as the natural satellites, as well as asteroids, comets and meteoroids.
